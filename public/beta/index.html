Weekly implementation breakdown

What happens each week.

The goal is simple: help construction owners see upcoming cash pressure before it happens, install a weekly cash decision rhythm, and create more confidence around spending, hiring, and growth decisions.

Week 1

Burn Rate Review

Estimated owner time commitment: 60–90 minutes

Goal: Understand current cash pressure and runway.

What Happens:

  • ✓ Review financial statements and current cash position
  • ✓ Input financial data into burn rate calculator
  • ✓ Estimate monthly and weekly burn
  • ✓ Discuss runway and slow season exposure
  • ✓ Review current cash pressure and owner observations

Outcome: Clear understanding of current cash pressure, runway, and financial exposure.

Week 2

Cash Trap Analysis

Estimated owner time commitment: 45–60 minutes

Goal: Identify where cash is getting stuck or leaking.

What Happens:

  • ✓ Review A/R delays and collection timing
  • ✓ Review payment timing and billing timing
  • ✓ Review deposits and draw structures
  • ✓ Discuss margin leaks and operational pressure points
  • ✓ Gather owner feedback and operational observations

Outcome: Identify the operational habits and timing issues creating unnecessary cash stress.

Week 3

13-Week Cash Visibility System

Estimated owner time commitment: 45–60 minutes

Goal: Build forward visibility.

What Happens:

  • ✓ Build weekly cash forecast
  • ✓ Review expected inflows and outflows
  • ✓ Identify future tight weeks and pressure points
  • ✓ Compare forecast versus actual cash movement
  • ✓ Establish a repeatable weekly review rhythm

Outcome: Ability to see upcoming cash pressure before decisions become emergencies.

Week 4

Cash Control Rules

Estimated owner time commitment: 30–45 minutes

Goal: Install repeatable operating discipline.

What Happens:

  • ✓ Implement and test rules around deposits and collections
  • ✓ Review payment timing and cash buffer targets
  • ✓ Discuss pipeline visibility and margin awareness
  • ✓ Review implementation difficulty and process clarity
  • ✓ Gather pricing expectations and business impact feedback

Outcome: More proactive control over cash movement and fewer avoidable surprises.
